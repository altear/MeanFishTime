The Apteronotus albifrons, or Black ghost knifefish, is a species of weakly electric fish that can sense its environment by releasing and receiving EM waves.

It has a special nervous system for releasing these signals. This system is one of the most accurate known biological clocks. The neurons that comprise it fire 100x more frequently than human neurons are capable of.

Using a passive sensor, we can capture this signal and turn it into a global clock.
